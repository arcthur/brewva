# Builder Mode Reference

Use this reference when `office-hours` is in side-project, hackathon,
open-source, learning, playful, or "I want to build something cool" mode.
Builder mode is not lower rigor. It optimizes for different currency.

## Principles

- Delight is the currency. The idea should create a "whoa", a habit, a useful
  artifact, or a story worth showing.
- Ship something you can show people. A visible, shareable slice beats an
  invisible architecture exercise.
- The best side projects often solve your own real problem. Personal itch is a
  valid demand signal when the goal is craft, learning, or open source.
- Explore before optimize. Do not turn a fun or learning project into a
  production-platform plan too early.
- Keep a first audience in mind: friends, contributors, future you, a specific
  community, a demo room, or a hackathon judge.

## Builder Questions

Ask one question at a time.

### Coolest Version

Ask: "What is the coolest version of this that would make someone say 'I want
to try that'?"

Use this to avoid flattening the idea into enterprise blandness.

### First Audience

Ask: "Who would you show it to first, and why would they care?"

The answer can be small: a teammate, a Discord community, a class, a repo
maintainer, or your future self.

### Fastest Shareable Slice

Ask: "What is the fastest path to something usable or shareable?"

Favor demos, examples, generated artifacts, CLI output, browser surfaces, or
one highly polished interaction.

### Existing Neighbor

Ask: "What is the closest existing thing, and what should feel different?"

This keeps the idea grounded while preserving originality.

### Ten-Times Version

Ask: "If time were unlimited, what would the 10x version unlock?"

Use this only after a shareable slice is clear. It should reveal direction, not
inflate the first build.

## Lateral Unlocks

Builder mode should propose at least one adjacent possibility when useful:

- turn a private tool into a public artifact
- make the output beautiful enough to share
- transform a workflow into a game, challenge, or benchmark
- expose a tiny API or plugin point that lets others play with it
- make a demo dataset, gallery, or before/after comparison

## When To Switch Modes

Switch toward startup mode when the user asks about market, customers, pricing,
sales, company formation, internal adoption, or whether this is a business.

Stay in builder mode when the stated goal is learning, fun, craft, reputation,
portfolio value, community usefulness, or hackathon/demo momentum.

## Output Signals

Strong builder diagnosis names:

- the delight hook
- the first audience
- the fastest shareable artifact
- the closest existing neighbor and desired difference
- one wild or lateral direction worth preserving
- the next assignment before implementation
