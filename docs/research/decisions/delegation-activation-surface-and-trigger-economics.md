# Decision: Delegation Activation Surface And Trigger Economics

## Metadata

- Decision: Brewva's delegation plane gains an ACTIVATION surface.
  Five generations hardened what happens after a delegation is requested and none
  built the signal economy that makes a capable model reach for a subagent; this
  adds it WITHOUT weakening governance. The operating contract and delegation tool
  guidelines flip from restrictive to affirmative scenario triggers; a render-time
  delegation advisory rides the hosted dynamic tail (pressure-relief and
  review-debt-closure reasons, silent unless actionable); core skills gain advisory
  delegation steps; the worker-adoption guidelines stop inducing a redundant merge
  probe; the unwired review-ensemble lane planner is deleted; and an operator report
  measures delegation trigger economics with a FAILURE-rate counter-signal.

## Decision Summary

- Activation is salience, not authority: doctrine, advisory, and skill steps supply
  delegation signals the model can bypass — no gate, no auto-spawn, and
  admission/adoption/replay decision points untouched (runtime decision points: 0).
- The advisory derives no gate: a render-time runtime-brief section, salience low,
  silent when a delegation is pending, when the gate would reject, or when there is
  no store — it recommends, it never refuses.
- Subtraction beats an unwired switch: the review-ensemble lane planner had zero
  producers and zero tests, superseded by requirement-fitness's single reviewer, so
  it is deleted rather than wired.
- Reliability is the counter-signal to activation: the report measures the
  delegation FAILURE rate, so a doctrine that lifts reach while dispatches fail is
  visible rather than mistaken for adoption.
- The instrument grades the hypotheses: the wording levers are activation hypotheses
  the report calibrates before and after; effectiveness is an empirical follow-up,
  the mechanisms are the accepted contract.

## Axioms

Obeys `docs/architecture/design-axioms.md`:

- Axiom 18 (load-bearing): doctrine, the advisory, skill steps, and the report are
  advisory surfaces that derive views, never authority; the advisory is
  model-bypassable and registered in the runtime-brief family.
- Axiom 1: attention belongs to the model — activation supplies salience inputs, it
  does not own salience (the skill-shortlist precedent).
- Axiom 4: govern effects, not thought paths — no runtime forces a spawn; the peer's
  plan-mode coercion is rejected, its authored-skill form kept.
- Axiom 3: subtraction beats switches — the unreachable lane planner is removed.
- Axiom 17: platform growth stays opt-in — no lever widens the default hosted path.
